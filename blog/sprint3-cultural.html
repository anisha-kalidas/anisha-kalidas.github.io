Emotional Intelligence

1. What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional intelligence (EQ) is the awareness that emotions can drive our behaviour and impact people in either a positive or negative way, and learning how to manage those emotions, especially when we are under pressure. People with high emotional intelligence are able to manage their emotions as well as use their emotions to facilitate their thinking and understand the emotions of others.

2. How is it different to IQ?

People with high IQs can think abstractly and make mental connections more easily. IQ is also used to identify people with very high intelligence or mental challenges.

3. Why is it important?

Developing your EQ will have major benefits to your ability to learn, perform and get hired. For one thing, these days we work largely in teams, not in isolation so realising and recognising emotions in ourselves and others can lead to healthier working environments. People with higher emotional intelligence are also more adaptable to change - a must in our fast-changing digital age.
